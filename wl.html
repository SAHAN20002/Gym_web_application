CORRECT WAY TO WEIGHT LIFTING

Weight lifting, also known as resistance training or strength training, is a form of exercise that focuses on improving muscle strength and endurance by lifting weights. It involves the use of free weights like dumbbells and barbells, as well as machines that provide resistance. Weight lifting is one of the most effective ways to build muscle mass, enhance physical performance, and improve overall fitness levels.

At the core of weight lifting is the principle of progressive overload, which refers to gradually increasing the weight or resistance you lift over time to stimulate muscle growth and strength. Exercises can target different muscle groups, such as the chest, back, legs, shoulders, and arms. Some common weight lifting exercises include bench presses, squats, deadlifts, shoulder presses, and bicep curls.

Weight lifting provides numerous health benefits beyond building muscle. It helps improve bone density, which is crucial for preventing osteoporosis, especially in older adults. It also promotes fat loss by increasing metabolism, as more muscle mass means your body burns more calories even at rest. Additionally, weight lifting enhances cardiovascular health by lowering blood pressure and improving cholesterol levels.Moreover, weight lifting contributes to better posture, flexibility, and balance, reducing the risk of injury in daily activities. It also has mental health benefits, such as reducing stress, anxiety, and depression, while boosting confidence and self-esteem.
